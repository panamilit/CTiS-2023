Artificial Reality, encompasses technology such as Virtual Reality and augmented reality, creating immersive digital environments and overlays that digital information onto the real world. Virtual Reality typically involves complete immersion in a virtual environment, while Augmented reality enhances the real world with digital elements, these technologies are used in various field, including entertainment, education, training, healthcare etc providing users with unique and interactive experiences that can simulate real-world scenarios or create entirely new ones.

Coexistence of lifestyle and artificial reality

Artificial reality technologies are increasingly being introduced into daily lifestyles, influencing how people work, learn, and interact. For example:

1. Entertainment: VR and AR are transforming the gaming industry, offering immersive experiences that go beyond traditional screen-based interactions
2. Education and training: VR simulations provide safe and realistic training environments for medical professionals, pilots, and other fields, enhancing learning and skill development
3. Fitness: VR-based workout programs and AR fitness apps make exercising more engaging and personalized, motivating people to maintain healthy habits
4. Social interaction: Virtual reality enables social interaction within digital spaces which allows people to connect and interact in new ways, regardless of physical distance
5. Work and productivity: remote work is enhanced by VR meetings and AR collaboration tools, making virtual workspaces more dynamic and interactive
